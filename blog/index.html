Bill Murray crashed my...log file

May 27th, 2014 by Greg

My business partner Stewart and I have worked on various products in the online F&B space over the years and experienced the successes and failures that come with startups. "The terrifying lows, the dizzying highs, the creamy middles!" as Homer Simpson said. Our latest product is eatabit.com where we are trying to solve the problem of how to transport food orders from the cloud, over the last mile of air, to a printed ticket inside a restaurant.

One of eatabit.com's applications is as a mobile F&B ordering system for large outdoor events and one of our clients is the Charleston Riverdogs, the minor league baseball team here in Charleston, SC.

We had a ton of challenges rolling out this side of the product: caching issues, printer firmware bugs, cellular network congestion and user training all took their turns beating us up. Over the last week, while the Riverdogs were playing away games, we persevered, pivoted and pirouetted our way through solutions to the technical issues.

It was exhausting.

Last night, the Riverdogs played the first of a series of home games against the Greenville Drive and we were onsite for tech support. Confident in the changes we had made, I still kept flipping tabs between New Relic and Papertrail, hoping not to see an error tear past on the screen...but it didn't. And the innings kept passing...

New Relic looking good...

Now back to Papertrail...wait, what?

Wow.

I have, as many in Charleston do, a handful of literally pedestrian Bill Murray encounters: I passed him on the sidewalk on my way in to work one morning; I think I saw him in a car at a stoplight one night... I knew he was part owner of the Riverdogs and frequented the games so why was seeing his name so rewarding?

Because just a day ago, we were stuck in the mud, under the water, short on air.

I smiled, closed the laptop and watched the end of the game.
